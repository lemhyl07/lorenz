Common Health Myths & Facts


Myth: You must drink 8 glasses of water a day.

Fact: Hydration varies by person, and thirst is the best indicator.

Myth: Cracking your knuckles causes arthritis.

Fact: No scientific evidence links cracking to arthritis.

Myth: You can catch a cold from being out in the cold.

Fact: Colds are caused by viruses, not temperature.

Myth: Detox diets cleanse your body.

Fact: Your liver and kidneys already detoxify naturally.

Myth: Eating late causes weight gain.

Fact: Weight gain depends on total calories, not the time you eat.

Myth: Vaccines cause autism.

Fact: There is no scientific link between vaccines and autism.

Myth: You can “sweat out” toxins.

Fact: Sweating does not remove toxins; your liver and kidneys do.

Myth: Carrots improve night vision dramatically.

Fact: Carrots are healthy but won’t drastically change night vision.

Myth: You need supplements to be healthy.

Fact: A balanced diet usually provides all necessary nutrients.

Myth: Natural sugar is always healthy.

Fact: Even natural sugars should be consumed in moderation.
